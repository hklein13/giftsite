The Four Questions

Discover Your Gift

These four questions form the foundation of the Gift Clarification Process. Take your time with each one, allowing space for honest reflection before moving to dialogue with others.

1

A Moment of Contribution

Recall a time you helped another person or contributed to a project where you felt genuinely useful — where you knew you were making a real difference. What were you doing? What made it feel meaningful?

2

Qualities You Admire

Think about someone you admire. What are three qualities or attributes you truly appreciate about that person? Often, the qualities we notice in others reflect something we carry within ourselves.

3

Qualities You Appreciate in Yourself

Identify three qualities that you appreciate about yourself. This can be challenging — we're often quicker to name our weaknesses. Take your time. What do you genuinely value about who you are?

4

Those Who See Your Best

If we were to invite three people into this conversation who have seen the "best of you" — people who have witnessed you at your most helpful, most alive, most yourself — who would they be? What might they say about your Gift?
