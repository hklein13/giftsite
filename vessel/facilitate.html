For Facilitators

Guide the Journey

Whether you're leading a family gathering or a team workshop, these guidelines will help you create a safe, meaningful space for Gift discovery.

The Facilitator's Role

As a facilitator, your job is not to analyze or interpret — it's to create the conditions for honest reflection and affirming dialogue. You are a guide, not an expert. The wisdom emerges from the participants themselves.

Before You Begin

Set the stage for meaningful conversation:

  • Choose a comfortable, private space free from distractions
  • Allow adequate time — rushing defeats the purpose
  • Ensure all participants understand the process beforehand
  • Consider starting with a meal or casual conversation to build connection

During the Process

Guide with gentleness and intention:

  • Read each question slowly, giving space for reflection
  • Encourage participants to share without judgment
  • Listen more than you speak
  • When witnessing, focus on specific examples and genuine observations
  • Allow silence — it's where insight often emerges

The Witnessing Phase

This is the heart of the process. When participants share what they see in each other:

  • Encourage specificity: "Tell me about a time you saw this in action"
  • Keep the focus positive and affirming
  • Help the recipient truly receive what is being offered
  • Notice the themes that emerge across different witnesses

Closing the Session

End with intention and forward momentum:

  • Help participants articulate a draft Gift Statement
  • Remind them this is a beginning, not an end
  • Encourage them to continue reflecting on what they heard
  • Consider scheduling a follow-up conversation

Create Safety

The most important thing you can do is create an atmosphere where people feel safe to be vulnerable and honest.

Trust the Process

The questions work. The witnessing works. Your job is to hold space, not to manufacture insights.

Model Presence

Be fully present. Put away devices. Give your complete attention to each speaker.
